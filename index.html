Fuel Consumption Among Vehicles

Understanding the impact of vehicle fuel consumption worldwide

Overview: Automotive Industry

Our first vehicle on this list are the figurehead of the automotive industry, cars. Big brands such as Toyota, Honda, Nissan, Stellantis (Chrysler group), and Ford are among the most unsustainable automotive brands in the industry, studies show (1). Most companies are already switching to more sustainable modes of energy, with Ford having a new line of electric cars such as the Mustang Mach-E, F-150 Lightning, and the e-Transit, having only the three cars so far (2). On the other side of the world, in China, however, companies are already past development and into production. Brands like Nio, BYD, and Xpeng are already mass-producing and selling electric cars as an alternative (3)(4). In comparison, Ford's F-150 Lightning boasts a 320-mile estimated range (5), while the BYD Shark 6 has a range of up to 522 miles (6).

Overview: Aviation Industry

Our second vehicle category on this list is commercial aircraft, which play a crucial role in global transportation but significantly impact environmental sustainability. Recent studies reveal substantial differences in efficiency among airlines and aircraft models. According to aviation sustainability research, Hainan Airlines has emerged as one of the industry leaders in fuel efficiency, while All Nippon Airways (ANA) ranks among the less sustainable carriers. The disparity becomes particularly evident when examining passenger efficiency metrics - Qantas, positioned on the lower end of the spectrum, transports just 22 passengers per kilometer on certain routes. Aircraft design innovations have dramatically improved fuel efficiency in recent decades. When comparing modern aircraft models, the double-decker Airbus A380, despite its impressive capacity, consumes approximately 3.1 liters of fuel per passenger per 100 kilometers, making it one of the least efficient aircraft in service. In contrast, the Boeing 787 Dreamliner sets new standards with its revolutionary composite materials and advanced aerodynamics, achieving remarkable fuel efficiency of about 2.1 liters per passenger per 100 kilometers. This represents a 20-25% improvement over previous generation aircraft.
